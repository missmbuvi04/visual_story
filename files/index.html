Outdoor Experiences

Our journey began with two powerful visits: the Nairobi National Museum and the Museum of Illusions.

At the National Museum, I stood in front of ancient artifacts and political history displays—stories I’d heard before but never truly felt. In the Hall of Human Origins, I was reminded that Kenya is the cradle of humankind. The Museum of Illusions, on the other hand, was playful and strange—it reminded me how perception can shift, just like our understanding of culture.

In contrast, Kereita Forest was quiet and grounding. Hiking under the trees, I found mental clarity. Nature is our greatest healer—and that’s something I took personally as someone deeply interested in mental health. The silence wasn’t empty; it was a reflection of rest, resilience, and restoration.
